It’s impossible to travel in Burma without visiting at least a few Buddhist pagodas and they are spottable almost every way you turn. Pagodas in Myanmar are used to describe all religious sites including temples and stupas. Stupa is a bell-shaped structure which contains religious relics such as a Buddha’s hair or tooth.There are famous pagodas in Myanmar. If you visit Mynamar,may I advice you to visit to the pagodas because many interesting places are in there and you can see ancient Myanmar architecture. Myanmar is famous for its plentiful pagodas such as Shwedagon pagoda, pagoda and Anada Temple.

Shwedagon Pagoda

Shwedagon Pagoda is also known as the Golden Pagoda. It is over 2,500 years old and a famous pagoda located in Yangon, Myanmar. Shwedagon pagoda is a national symbol as well as a sacred site for Buddhists all over the world. The exterior of the pagoda is covered with the priceless gems, gold plates and complete with a diamond-studded spire. The pagoda contains four valuable relics, all of which are thought to have belonged to three previous Buddhas and the current Buddha, Gautama. The pagoda consists of hundreds of other temples, stupas and statues, all with incredible details. As Shwedagon pagoda is the most sacred place for Buddhists in Myanmar, large numbers of devotees come to Shwedagon every day.They walk around the stupa, pray the pagoda and offering flowers to the Buddha.

Maharmuni Pagoda

Maharmuni Pagoda is the most important religious site in Mandalay, Myanmar. Maharmuni Pagoda is enshrined in a small chamber topped with a seven tiered Pyatthat, Burmese style roof. To pay respect to the pagoda, male devotees apply gold leaf to the pagoda, which is called shwe cha in Burmese. Visitors can see the magnificent and skillful sculpture status as well as study many fascinating tradions. Besides praying and worshiping, there are also other rituals at Maharmuni. Every early morning, the monks brush the statue with a brush which is made of Neem trees and wiping the face with perfumed water. There is famous festival held at the Manarmuni Buddha Temple in early January every year to celebrate the history of the temple's formation and development. There is also a small market where visitors can find the most beautiful Burmese marionettes for souvenir.

Kyaik Hti Yo Pagoda

Kyaik Hti Yo Pagoda, also called the Golden Rock is one of the most sacred Buddhist sites in Myanmar. The pagoda is located close to Kyaikto town, in Mon State. A massive boulder on the edge of a cliff with a pagoda is enshrining a hair of the Buddha. The rock balances on the edge of a cliff and it appears like it can fall down in the valley below it at any moment. The rock is covered with gold leaf applied to the rock by Buddhist male devotees. As Kyaik Hti Yo Pagoda is one of the most religious places of pilgrimage for Buddhists, many people come to the pagoda. Everyday, they light the candles and incense sticks, chant the prayers and offering the flowers to the pagoda. The full moon day of Tabaung in March, the platform of the pagoda is lighted with ninty thousand candles as reverential offering to the Buddha.

Mingun Pagoda

The massive pagoda, also known as the Mingun Pahtodawgyi is located on the banks of the Irrawaddy river, in Mandalay. At the center of the fifty meter high pagoda facing the river is a huge richly decorated entrance. The Mingun pagoda is a massive unfinished pagoda built at the end of the 18th century. Inside the pagoda is a small shrine with a Buddha image.

Mingun Bell

In 1808 King Bodawpaya had an enormous bell made that was meant to be installed at the top of the giant stupa. The bell is almost 4 meters and largest bell in the world. The number 55555 is inscribed in Burmese script (Min Phyu man man Pyaw) on the outside of the bell, 55555 being the weight in viss, a Burmese unit of measurement, which is about 90 tons.

Mya Thein Tan Pagoda

Mya Thein Tan Pagoda

The Mya Thein Tan pagoda is also known as the Hsinbyume pagoda is located on the banks of the Irrawaddy river just North of Mandalay, nearby Mingun Pagoda. The pagoda is a beautiful all white structure. The pagoda’s architectural style is very different from other pagodas in Myanmar. The pagoda that is topped with a gold spire enshrines a Buddha image.

Kuthodaw Pagoda

Kuthodaw Pagoda

The Kuthodaw pagoda comprises of a gilded pagoda, hundreds of shrines housing inscribed marble slabs and several pavilions. It is located in Mandalay. The pagoda is also called “The world’s largest book”, named after the 729 marble slabs inscribed with Buddhist teachings, each stone slab representing one of its pages.

Dhamayangyi Temple

Dhammayangyi Temple is the largest temple in Bagan. It was built by King Narathu in 1170. Narathu became King of Bagan after murdering his father and his brother. Narathu was worried about bad fortune and built to massive temple to gain merit and to compensate for murdering his father and his brother. Dhammayangyi temple was never fully completed because construation was stopped after the death of King Narathu. The brick of Dhammayangyi was done with great precision and worth visit.

Anada Pagoda

Anada Temple is one of the finest and most venerated temples in bagan. Anada pagoda is a single storey structure built towards the end of the early Bagan period. It was built by King Kyanzittha in 1091. The temple's most distinctive feature is the gilded shikhara, the tower like spire on top of the pagoda. The reflection of the gilded shikhara is visible from miles away over the plains. Ananda Pagoda festival is held yearly around January. Many visitors come Ananda Temple to see the unique and masterpiece architecture of Bagan.
