Inlay Lake

Inle Lake is a large freshwater lake surrounded by hills in the Shan state of central Myanmar. The shallow lake that is about 18 kilometers in length is a place of scenic beauty with a relaxed, tranquil atmosphere. The lake is known for its floating gardens and “leg rowers” because of their distinctive style of rowing, standing on one leg, attached to the other leg an oar, which they paddle in the waters.

Five days floating market

The main town of the Inle Lake area is Nyaungshwe, connected to the lake by a kilometers long canal. Its main attractions are the morning market and a number of Buddhist monasteries. The people who live in Inlay grow crops as tomatoes and flowers in floating gardens on the lake. They sell their crops at the “5 day market”, which is held in a five day rotating cycle in five villages around the lake. A few kilometers South East of Nyaungshwe is the Red estate vineyard. Visitors can taste several locally produced of wines.

Phaung Daw Oo Pagoda

Phaung Daw Oo pagoda is one of the holiest sites in Shan State and the shrine is huge and features five ancient golden Buddhas. The pagoda is easily reached by boat from Inlay Lake. At the center of the temple is a golden stupa topped with an ornamental hti. The interior walls of the temple are decorated with murals depicting Buddhist stories. Every day Buddhist devotees come to the pagoda to pay their respect to the images and apply more gold leaf, which only men are allowed to do. And visitors can receive the generous smiles by local people.

Phaung Daw Oo Pagoda Festival

Phaung Daw Oo pagoda festival is held during the Burmese month of Thadingyut (October/November). The Karaweik boat carry four of the five images in procession across the lake during the Phaung Daw Oo Pagoda festival. At the front of the boat is the large gilded head of a Karaweik bird and at the back is the gilded tail of the mythological bird. This festival is the fundamental one to visit for not only visitors but also locals because of the renowned activity, rowing competitions between the villages, using the local one leg rowing style.

Lotus Weaving

Lotus weaving is an exquisite handicraft of Intha people that will give you an exceptional experience in your Inle Lake tour. For a long time, the Intha has made their living from the beautiful lotus stems on the largest freshwater of Myanmar and creating the world’s most precious and finest fabric from lotus.

Lotus Fibre

As lotus silk is one of Myanmar’s iconic textiles, visitors can only find lotus woven products in Inle Lake, Shan State. The material of lotus silk is unlike any other. It is known for its versatile functions, which the wearer will be kept warm in the cold weather, and cool when it is hot. It also never wrinkles. An elegant piece of handwoven lotus fibre fabric that is smooth and soft to the touch. This exotic textile can be sold at a very high price, depending on its quality and detail. Lotus silk is not only a precious heritage that makes up part of Myanmar’s identity, but it also holds a traditional value to the people of Inlay.
