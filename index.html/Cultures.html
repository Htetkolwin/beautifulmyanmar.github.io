Myanmar is a popular destination for not only the ancient beauty of sceneries, mysterious features but also the beauty of culture, customs, and festivals.

Novititate Ceremony (Shin Pyu)

Novitiate as known as Shin Pyu in Myanmar is an important ceremony in Burmese life. It marks the event of a young boy being a monk in a short period of time. According to Burmese culture, people want their children to enter the temple and become a monk. They believe the child will get luck and the family will be blessed in life.

Novititate Ceremony

Early in the morning, a group of about 15 children, both male and female (5-15 years old), made up by their families, wearing traditional Myanmar costumes, and gathered at a place in the village to start the ritual. Then the organizer will announce the ceremony. After the announcement ceremony, the children will ride colorful horses and carried around the village or town.

Burmese Thanakha

Thanaka is a traditional powder derived from the bark of certain trees growing in the Central regions of Myanmar. People grind the tree bark against a flat and wet circular smooth stone then put the paste on to the face, mainly the cheeks for skin care purpose. Myanmar women like to apply it on the face after bathing.

Thanakha

Thanakha

Applying the face with Thanakha is an important custom in the traditional culture of Myanmar people. They apply the Thanakha to protect the skin from sunlight and cool the skin. By applying the Thanakha, face is smooth and protect from sunlight.

Burmese Marionettes Dance

Puppetry is an ancient art form or theatre performance which involves manipulation of marionettes. Burmese marionette troupe must have 27 characters, including a king, animals such as horse, elephant, tiger, monkey and parrot, ministers, prince and princess and buffoons. A saing waing, a traditional Burmese orchestra usually provides the music. It accentuates the dramatic moments of the performance.

Marionettes with puppeteer

Prince Marionette & Princess Marionette

Burmese marionettes are very intricate and dexterous as they perform 18 (for male characters) or 19 (for female) wires. The puppets were usually made of wood and the type of wood would depend on the kind of character. The wood has to be resistant, easy to carve and light.
